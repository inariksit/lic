Properties of SAT-CG

  • Rules apply in parallel
    • Two schemes to emulate ordering
  • Careful context irrelevant
  • Rules disambiguate more
    • REMOVE v IF (-1 det) may remove det

Evaluation

  • Traditional grammars written with order in mind
    • Traditional CG-engines do better with those grammars
  • SAT-CG 10 to 100 times slower than state of the art
  • Potential for smaller grammars?

Analysing CG with SAT

Examples of conflicts

Rules R and r conflict, if

  • after applying R, it is impossible to apply r, regardless of input
  • R can also be a list of rules.

...regardless of input

No conflict: There is a sequence where r can remove something, after R. Conflict: No such sequence can be constructed.
